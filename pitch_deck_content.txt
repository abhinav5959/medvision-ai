SLIDE 2: PROBLEM STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[Key Points]
• Diagnostic Delay & Errors: In busy emergency departments, patients wait hours 
  for an on-call radiologist to review X-rays. 
• Missed Fractures: Missed or misdiagnosed fractures (especially hairline or 
  non-displaced fractures) make up to 80% of all diagnostic errors in emergency 
  medicine.
• Severe Consequences: Diagnostic delays or missed fractures lead to chronic pain, 
  improper healing (malunion), and expensive medical malpractice liabilities.
• Demographic Impact: Community clinics, rural hospitals, and underfunded centers 
  face acute shortages of imaging specialists.

[Supporting Facts & Stats]
• Up to 10% of emergency department bone fractures are initially missed or misread.
• Musculoskeletal conditions affect over 1.7 billion people globally.
• Radiologist burnout has increased by 40% over the last decade, leading to longer 
  backlogs.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 3: ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[Key Causes]
• Manual Triaging Bottlenecks: Emergency physicians must triage scans without 
  immediate specialist verification.
• Plain Film Limitations: Subtle cracks (e.g. hairline, greenstick, oblique) 
  are difficult to detect with the naked eye under poor lighting.
• "Black-Box" AI Hesitancy: Existing AI screening tools output a simple "Yes/No" 
  percentage. Doctors do not trust tools that cannot explain *why* they flagged a scan.
• Input Quality Variation: Blurry, dark, low-resolution, or cropped scans 
  frequently break model predictions, leading to unreliable results.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 4: PROPOSED SOLUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[Overview]
MedVision AI is an explainable, model-agnostic, and production-ready decision-support 
API that combines Computer Vision with Large Language Models to deliver safe, transparent, 
and multi-disciplinary diagnostic reports.

[Key Features]
1. Image Quality Gatekeeper: Real-time OpenCV analysis checks resolution, contrast, 
   blur, and brightness, blocking bad inputs before inference.
2. Dual-Stage Vision Pipeline: Custom ResNet-50 models run binary fracture detection 
   and 12-class fracture classification (e.g., Comminuted, Spiral, Hairline).
3. Grad-CAM Explainability: Highlights the exact coordinates where the model 
   visualizes structural fractures.
4. Structured Medical Debate: Generates distinct clinical perspectives from 
   virtual specialist personas (Radiologist, Surgeon, Emergency Physician).
5. Interactive Reasoning Timeline: Visualizes the pipeline's diagnostic stages 
   ("What Changed My Mind?").

[Value Proposition]
Empowers non-specialist physicians with real-time, explainable diagnostic confidence 
and patient stabilization guidelines within seconds.

SLIDE 9: CHALLENGES & RISK MITIGATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[Technical Risks & Mitigation]
• Model Hallucinations: 
  *Mitigation*: Gemini is strictly constrained using Pydantic structured output 
  schemas based directly on PyTorch vision model inputs.
• Input Noise / Bad Scans:
  *Mitigation*: OpenCV pre-inference quality checks reject blurry or low-resolution 
  images before they reach the models.
• Low-Memory Cloud Server Crashes:
  *Mitigation*: Re-architected model lifecycle to run sequential loading and unloading, 
  avoiding concurrent RAM spikes.

[Security & Privacy]
• HIPAA Compliance: De-identifies images on upload; no Patient Health Information (PHI) 
  is processed or retained.
